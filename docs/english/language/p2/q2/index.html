Summary of the differences between the behaviour of the children at the two schools

Posted: 2021-02-05   |   Tags: english, language, paper two, question two

In Source A, the attitudes to education are open, with a lot of emphasis on creativity. This can be inferred from the fact that “children’s painting[s] and poems […] covered every available space”, suggesting that the school embraced the children’s creativity. However, in the second source, the teacher remarks the schoolgirls are “boxing most viciously”, showing that the school is a place of violence rather than education.

In the first source, the the children are described as “bright-eyed and keen”. This emphasises that the children want to learn and enjoy the process. Whereas, in Source B, the children in the school are “ready for anything but work”, giving the impression that they don’t want to be at school, nor do they want to work.

The primary school’s headmistress “boasted” about the children’s abilities, showing her deep faith in them. In contrast, in Source B, the teacher has “half-a-dozen at least” stones thrown at her, and a boy in ready with a “brick in his hand, to have his revenge”. The children “have ever considered themselves at war” with the teachers, showing that the teacher has to dodge the violence towards her.

Despite both school’s having industrial settings, the Phinn writes that “from the grime and dust, I walked into an oasis”, showing the contrasts between the surroundings and a place of education. In the ragged school, the narrator remarks that “no school can be possibly worst than this”. The lanes into school are a “moral hell”, and this attitude practically extends into the school as well.
