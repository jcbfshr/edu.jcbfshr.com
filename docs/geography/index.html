Evaluate the environmental impact of extraction of raw materials in Nigeria

Posted: 2021-02-07   |   Tags: environment, geography, nigeria, oil extraction, raw materials

(revisions in bold) Oil extraction can lead to oil spills which are highly damaging to local ecosystems, such as the Bodo oil spills between 2008-2009. Because of the hugely dense deposit of oil (11 million gallons across 20km²), it will take at least 30 years for the area’s ecosystem to regenerate. The damage can be amplified in undersea oil pipelines and offshore oil pumps as special diving equipment or specialised for bigger oil spills, which take a long time to deploy and, therefore, to fix the oil spill. Reducing the North-South divide

Posted: 2021-02-06   |   Tags: geography, north-south divide, UK

(revisions in bold) The North-South divide is stark, yet the line seems impalpable to most everyone. Part of the reason for this is that the changes between the “north” and the “south” occur gradually — despite many trying to clearly define a line similar to Hadrian’s Wall. South-west Stonehenge "super highway"

Posted: 2021-02-06   |   Tags: geography, stonehenge, UK

In 2017, Theresa May’s government announced plans to build a tunnel, at least 3.2km long, underneath Stonehenge, but 50m further away from the monument, although the first mention of a tunnel under Stonehenge came in 1995. This plan is a result of the £15 billion road improvement plan that Cameron’s government announced in 2014, under which the A303 was flagged requiring improvements.
