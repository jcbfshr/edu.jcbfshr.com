Compare how the writers convey their different attitudes to the two schools

Posted: 2021-02-25   |   Tags: english, language, paper two, question four

In both sources, the surroundings are abysmal, yet the staff share very different attitudes to the school. In Source A, Sister Brendan is eager to show off her fantastic pupils to the inspector, whereas in Source B, the writer dreads school. The writer in Source A shows Sister Brendan’s zeal in the way she moves: she “glided through the school”. The onomatopoeic verb “glide” shows that the writer picks up on how at ease she feels around the children, and how she feels comfortable among them, like a blade of grass in a field. Read more...

Summary of the differences between the behaviour of the children at the two schools

Posted: 2021-02-05   |   Tags: english, language, paper two, question two

In Source A, the attitudes to education are open, with a lot of emphasis on creativity. This can be inferred from the fact that “children’s painting[s] and poems […] covered every available space”, suggesting that the school embraced the children’s creativity. However, in the second source, the teacher remarks the schoolgirls are “boxing most viciously”, showing that the school is a place of violence rather than education.
